PONGAL

Ingredients:

For Pongal:

1.1 cup raw rice

2.1/4 cup split yellow moong dal (lentils)

3.2-3 cups water (adjust as needed)

4.1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns

5.1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds

6.1/2 inch piece of ginger, finely chopped or grated

7.A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

8.2-3 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)

9.2-3 tablespoons cashew nuts

10.Salt to taste

For Tempering:

1-2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)

1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds

1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds

A few curry leaves

2-3 dry red chilies (optional)

A pinch of asafoetida (hing)

Method to cook:

Wash the rice and lentils together and set them aside.

In a large, heavy-bottomed pan or pressure cooker, heat 2-3 tablespoons of ghee over medium heat.

Add black peppercorns, cumin seeds, grated ginger, and a pinch of asafoetida. Sauté for a minute or until they become fragrant.

Add the washed rice and lentils to the pan and sauté for another 2-3 minutes, ensuring they are well-coated with ghee and the spices.

Pour in 2-3 cups of water (adjust the quantity to your preferred consistency), add salt to taste, and mix well.

If using a pressure cooker, close the lid without the weight and cook for 2-3 whistles. If using a regular pan, cover and simmer on low heat, stirring occasionally, until the rice and lentils are soft and cooked. This might take around 20-25 minutes.

While the pongal is cooking, in a separate small pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee. Add cashew nuts and fry them until they turn golden brown. Remove and set aside.

In the same pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves, dry red chilies (if using), and a pinch of asafoetida for tempering. Allow them to splutter and become aromatic.

Once the pongal is cooked, add the tempered spices to the pongal and mix well.

Finally, add the fried cashew nuts, and your Pongal is ready to serve.

PASTA

Ingredients:

For the Pasta:

1.8 ounces (about 2 cups) of pasta (e.g., spaghetti, fettuccine, penne)

2.Water for boiling

3.Salt (for boiling water)

4.Tomato sauce

Instructions:

Boil the Pasta:

Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add a generous pinch of salt to the boiling water.

Add the pasta to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions or until it reaches your desired level of doneness (usually about 8-12 minutes).

Stir occasionally to prevent sticking. When the pasta is done, it should be al dente, meaning it's cooked but still slightly firm to the bite.

Drain the cooked pasta in a colander and set it aside.

Transfer the pasta to serving plates.

Garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil leaves if desired.

Serve hot and enjoy your homemade pasta!
